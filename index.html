Knox Planning Scheme · Building Regulations 2018

Ten rules govern this rebuild — the design passes all ten

The lot sits in NRZ4 with no overlays. Because it is over 300 m², a single new dwelling needs no planning permit — only a building permit, with siting checked by a private building surveyor against Part 5 of the Building Regulations. The mandatory “garden area” is a legal open-space quota (lawn and paving qualify) — it does not require any planted garden, matching the brief: no pool, no garden beds, maximum house.

Maximised within the envelope

“Pimpala 63” — a 588 m² two-storey family home

An L-shaped plan squeezes the most floor area the rules allow on this trapezoidal block: a 13.0 × 18.5 m main wing hugging the deep east boundary at 1.2 m, and a 7.0 × 14.0 m garage wing at 1.0 m off the west boundary. The backyard is plain lawn and a paved terrace — nothing to maintain, and still 42 % garden area against the mandatory 35 %.
